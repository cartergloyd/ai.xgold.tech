Automation wedge

Start with exception queues.

Late pickup, no tracking, detention, rejected tender, missing POD, accessorial mismatch, and hot-load escalation are concrete enough to automate and measure.

Humans

The Escalation Layer

Human-owned work

  • High-value shipper updates, recovery plans, and service failure messaging
  • Carrier negotiation, fall-off recovery, accessorial disputes, and detention approvals
  • Priority calls when hot freight, premium customers, and margin collide
  • Customer-specific judgment around scorecards, routing guides, and service commitments

GoldAI Design Rule

Reps should handle judgment, negotiation, and trust moments. They should not spend the day refreshing FourKites, checking DAT, copying emails into McLeod, or chasing routine check-call updates.

AI Agents

The Freight Interpretation Layer

Agent-owned work

  • Interpreting carrier emails, SMS replies, ELD visibility gaps, and customer portal notes
  • Summarizing load history across TMS notes, tracking events, CRM activity, and email threads
  • Drafting shipper updates with ETA, cause, recovery action, and confidence level
  • Recommending whether to re-cover, escalate, wait, or notify the customer

Common tools

McLeod, MercuryGate, Aljex, Rose Rocket, Tai TMS, Turvo, project44, FourKites, MacroPoint, DAT, Truckstop, Highway, MyCarrierPackets, RMIS, Salesforce, Outlook, Teams.

Scripts

The Freight Execution Layer

Script-owned work

  • Stale tracking thresholds, geofence misses, tender age, and appointment-risk triggers
  • Load status writebacks, task creation, queue assignment, and customer-update cadence
  • BOL, POD, rate confirmation, lumper, detention, and accessorial document validation
  • Carrier onboarding checks, insurance expiration flags, and lane-specific rule enforcement

GoldAI Design Rule

If the trigger and next action are known, use scripts. AI should not be paid to do threshold checks, task creation, or status writebacks a deterministic workflow can run all day.

Data & Systems

The Load Record Layer

System-owned truth

  • Load status, tender status, pickup/delivery appointment, carrier, lane, and shipper records
  • Visibility events, check-call history, EDI updates, and customer portal commitments
  • CRM notes, routing-guide promises, escalation history, and account-specific rules
  • Billing, POD, accessorial, detention, lumper, and claims evidence

GoldAI Design Rule

The load record wins. Automation must update the TMS and CRM trail instead of creating another offline tracker that operations stops trusting by Friday.

Exception Intake & Triage

  1. HOps defines exception classes: late pickup, missed delivery, no tracking, detention, rejected tender, OS&D, rate dispute
  2. AAgent reads carrier email, EDI 214 alerts, visibility events, customer portal comments, and TMS notes
  3. SScript classifies severity by SLA, appointment window, customer tier, lane, margin, and owner
  4. DTMS, CRM, visibility, and customer-facing notes update with status and next action

Carrier Follow-Up Loop

  1. STrigger fires when tracking is stale, geofence confidence drops, or appointment risk increases
  2. AAgent drafts carrier-specific follow-up using load number, lane, facility, hours, and prior replies
  3. DResponses are parsed from email, SMS, portal notes, ELD/visibility tools, or dispatcher updates
  4. HCoordinator handles disputes, re-cover decisions, customer-sensitive exceptions, or unresolved risk

Customer Status Updates

  1. DLoad status, ETA, facility appointment, dwell, and exception context are pulled from source systems
  2. AAgent summarizes what changed, operational impact, recovery path, and confidence level
  3. SUpdate is posted to CRM, email, customer portal, Teams channel, or shared exception queue
  4. HAccount owner approves updates for strategic accounts, service failures, or scorecard-sensitive freight

Rate & Document Cleanup

  1. AAgent extracts shipment details from rate confirmations, BOLs, PODs, lumper receipts, and detention forms
  2. SScript validates shipper, consignee, PO, weight, accessorial, and appointment fields against TMS records
  3. DMissing data, accessorials, OS&D notes, and exceptions are written back for billing or claims review
  4. HBilling, claims, or ops resolves mismatches above customer or dollar threshold
